Ambiguous openings: There was, It were, It was. These sentence openings are vague. The more specific your writing, the more authority your document will have.

Ambiguous pronouns: It, these, those, and that are vague. The more precise your writing, the better.

Passive voice: Was reading, is happening, are reading. A to be verb followed by a verb is an indicator of passive voice. Try to write 90% of your document in active voice.

Subjunctive mood: Would of, should of, could of. Besides sounding weak, subjunctive mood can also suggest a condition when it is not present. This can cause confusion.

Words that end with ly: Basically, usually, normally. These words can usually be removed from your document without losing any meaning.
